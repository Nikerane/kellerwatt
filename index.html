The problem

Germany's grid is breaking at both ends.

Renewables are flooding in faster than the grid can store them. At the same time, 58% of Germans live in buildings with no energy assets at all.

There is a battery-shaped hole in the middle of the market — and nobody is filling it.

The solution

A battery in every basement.

We lease unused basement space, install a 200 kWh battery on its own grid meter, and let a licensed trading partner do the rest — fully automated, with zero tenant involvement.

STEP 01

We lease the space

A 10–15 year lease with the owner or WEG. We pay €800–1,200 a year; tenants can see a lower Hausgeld.

STEP 02

We install the battery

A 200 kWh LFP system with built-in fire suppression, smart meter and a dedicated grid connection. VDE-certified.

STEP 03

A partner trades it

Our licensed BKV partner buys when power is cheap or negative and sells at peak. Fully automated. No tenant involvement, ever.

STEP 04

Revenue stacks up

Spot arbitrage, FCR grid services and a §14a EnWG tariff reduction combine to €8,000–9,700 gross per unit each year.

For building owners

Built for owners who'd rather not think about energy.

Passive income

A fixed annual lease for space you weren't using. No capital outlay, no maintenance — we carry all of it. Many landlords pass savings on as a lower Hausgeld.

Zero tenant involvement

Nothing changes for residents. The battery sits quietly in the basement — VDE-certified, fire-suppressed, the same risk profile as a gas boiler.

Climate & grid impact

Every unit helps absorb renewable energy the grid can't yet store — and earns measurable ESG credit for your building.
